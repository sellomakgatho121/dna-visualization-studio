4-Strand DNA (G-Quadruplex)

  • G-Quadruplex Structure: Four DNA strands arranged in a square formation
  • G-Tetrads: Guanine bases form square planar structures (G-quartets)
  • Central Cation: Potassium (K+) or sodium (Na+) ions stabilize the structure
  • Hoogsteen Base Pairing: Bases form hydrogen bonds in the central cavity
  • 90Â° Geometry: Strands positioned at 90-degree intervals
  • Biological Role: Found in telomeres, promoter regions, and involved in gene regulation

G-quadruplexes are naturally occurring four-stranded DNA structures that play important roles in telomere maintenance, gene expression regulation, and are associated with cancer and neurological disorders.
